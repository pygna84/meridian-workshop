What we heard

  • Operations runs the business through a dashboard that isn't carrying its weight
  • Reports module has known defects — eight logged, likely more
  • Restocking decisions still happen in spreadsheets
  • IT has paused changes because there's no test coverage
  • Previous vendor left work unfinished, documentation thin

Why this matters

Three warehouses. ~180 people. ~$9.6M revenue running through this dashboard daily.

Tokyo team is locked out of i18n. London and SF live with broken Reports. Tanaka's team plans restocking in Excel.

Every week the dashboard stays broken is a week of operational tax.

Our approach — three principles

Unblock IT first

Tests in week 2, not month 5. Every fix lands behind a green build.

Fix before adding

R1 Reports remediation before R2 Restocking. Visible wins inside the first month.

Document as we go

R4 architecture doc starts week 1, lives in the repo, refreshed continuously.

Surface risk in writing

Weekly status, named risks, no surprises in week 12.

The sequencing call

Meridian listed R3 (testing) fourth. We'd ship it first.

IT is the gatekeeper. Without tests, Reports fixes recreate the previous vendor's pattern: code lands, regressions follow, IT pushes back.

Tests aren't the polish — they're the unlock.

Where the schedule lives or dies

The single longest dependency:

R2 design checkpoint (week 3) → R2 build (weeks 7–12) → UAT (weeks 15–16)

If Tanaka's team can drive a one-hour design session in week 3, the rest of the plan holds.

We name this risk in the proposal, not in week 8.

Commercials

T&M with Not-To-Exceed — phased ceilings, no rolling between phases.

  • Pay for actual time, capped per phase
  • If we approach a ceiling, you hear about it in writing — before we hit it
  • Underspend stays with Meridian
  • Change control: written, with three-day turnaround. Sub-half-day fixes don't generate paperwork.
